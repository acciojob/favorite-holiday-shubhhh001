Bramhagiri Hills


Brahmagiri Hill is an important and sacred hill located near Nashik in Maharashtra. It is best known as the origin of the Godavari River, one of the longest and holiest rivers in India. The hill is part of the Western Ghats and is surrounded by rich greenery, making it a popular destination for pilgrims, trekkers, and nature lovers. At the top of Brahmagiri Hill lies the Trimbakeshwar Temple, a famous Jyotirlinga of Lord Shiva, which adds great religious significance to the place. Due to its spiritual importance, natural beauty, and historical value, Brahmagiri Hill holds a special place in the cultural heritage of Nashik district.
